Just Fucking Use Cron.

You do not need a distributed workflow orchestration platform with a mascot. You need a schedule and a command. Relax.

Golden rule:
If your requirement is “run this thing every X minutes,” your requirement is not “introduce a control plane.” Your requirement is a timer.

What cron is great at

  • Daily, hourly, weekly jobs that do one thing and exit.
  • Cleanup, rollups, exports, backups, reports.
  • Calling a URL because the business said so.
  • Letting you sleep instead of babysitting YAML.

What you’re probably doing instead

You built a scheduler inside your app.

Now deploys change schedules and crashes skip jobs. You reinvented cron. Worse.

You added a workflow platform.

Because “run daily” felt too simple and you missed diagrams.

You run jobs on every instance.

Your “daily email” sends six times. Users love that.

You have no alerts.

The job failed weeks ago. Now it’s folklore.

Examples

# every day at 02:15
15 2 * * * /usr/local/bin/report >> /var/log/report.log 2>&1

# every 5 minutes
*/5 * * * * /usr/local/bin/healthcheck

Symptoms you are overengineering

  • You used the word orchestrate in a standup.
  • Your “job” has a UI.
  • You needed a blog post to explain how it runs.
  • You added retries before idempotency.
  • You said “it’s basically cron, but…”

When cron is not enough

  • Long-running, multi-step workflows with human approvals.
  • Massive fan-out with real distributed coordination.
  • Millions of per-tenant schedules.
Final reminder:
If your job needs a SDK, UI, control plane, and conference talk, you are not scaling.

You are avoiding a crontab.
